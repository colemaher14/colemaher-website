Prescription Importation Expansion & Medication Costs

Policy Analysis | May 7, 2025

This comprehensive analysis examines how expanding prescription drug importation could alleviate medication costs in the United States, particularly benefiting vulnerable populations disproportionately affected by pharmaceutical pricing.

"Elderly populations, though typically insured through Medicare, frequently report challenges affording essential medications due to complex prescription regimens and fixed incomes."

Principal Findings:

  • Prescription costs disproportionately burden seniors, low-income individuals, and minorities
  • Current importation policies exclude critical medications like insulin
  • Strategic importation could strengthen price negotiation leverage
  • Potential for significant Medicare/Medicaid savings
  • Requires safeguards against international price fluctuations
